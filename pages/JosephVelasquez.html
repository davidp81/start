Joseph Velasquez

Teacher of Screenprinting, Relief, and Intaglio.

Featured Lecturer at over 145 Universities

Joseph Velasquez was born in Texas and raised in California. He earned his MFA in printmaking at the University of Wisconsin, Madison, and received his BFA from the University of Mary-Hardin Baylor, Belton, Texas. He is the co-creator of a mobile printmaking studio, Drive By Press, that has travelled over 200,000 miles across the country and has been to 145 Universities where he has lectured and demonstrated printmaking practices.

Teacher of Screenprinting

He is a National Endowment for the Arts recipient: Funded through The John Michael Kohler Arts Center for a Community Arts Project and residency. He is currently an Artist and Professor at Florida Atlantic University where he teaches Screenprinting, Relief, and Intaglio.

Exhibits featured at

Velasquez’s work has been exhibited at numerous national and international venues, notably "Air Land Seed” and “Epicentro: Re Tracing the Plains” curated by Nancy Marie Mithlo on the occasion of the Venice Biennale 54th and 55th International Art Exhibition, Venice, Italy; South African Museum, Cape Town, South Africa; The Print Center, Philadelphia, Pennsylvania; Doujiao Museum of Art, Songhuang Art Village, Beijing China; and a highlight for him as Artist and Curator, Crossing State Lines: A Survey of American Printmakers, University of Dundee, Scotland, UK. Exhibition of prints from American artists Velasquez met on his mobile printmaking tour.

Submission Guidelines

We accept submissions only through our website submission system. We do not accept submissions through postal services or email. You may send us PDFs (under 10mb) including pictures of your art work and biography. If you do not have your work in PDF format, you may include a link to your works in the message box.

Links to personal websites, Instagram, Behance, etc... will all be accepted. PDF is not required. Please include your full name and email along with submission.
